Chemistry

Chemistry is the study of matter and the waves in which different form of matters combine with each other.

There are eight braches of chemistry.

  • Physical chemistry
  • Organic chemistry
  • Inorganic chemistry
  • Biochemistry
  • Analytical chemistry
  • Environmental chemistry
  • Industrial chemistry
  • Nuclear chemistry
Physical chemistry:
The branch of chemistry concerned with the application of the techniques and theories of physics to the study of chemical systems.
Organic chemistry:
Organic chemistry is the study of the structure, properties, composition, reactions, and preparation of carbon-containing compounds.
Inorganic chemistry:
Inorganic chemistry is concerned with the properties and behavior of inorganic compounds, which include metals, minerals, and organometallic compounds.
Biochemistry:
Biochemistry is the application of chemistry to the study of biological processes at the cellular and molecular level.
Analytical chemistry:
Analytical chemistry is the science of obtaining, processing, and communicating information about the composition and structure of matter,
Environmental chemistry:
Environmental chemistry is the study of the effects that chemicals have on the air, water and soil and how they impact the environment and human health.
Industrial chemistry:
Industrial chemistry is the process of transforming matter into materials that are useful to humanity through chemical processes.
Nuclear chemistry:
Nuclear chemistry is the subdiscipline of chemistry that is concerned with changes in the nucleus of elements.
