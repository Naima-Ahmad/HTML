A karahi is a type of thick, circular, and deep cooking pot that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It is used in Indian, Afghan, Pakistani, Bangladeshi/Bengali, Nepalese and Caribbean cuisines. Traditionally press-formed from mild steel sheet or made of wrought iron, a karahi resembles a wok with steeper sides.

Chicken karahi is a poultry dish inger, allspice, cardamom, tomatoes, and garlic.

When prepared with mutton, the dish is known as gosht karahi. It is traditionally served with rice, roti, or naan.
